THE COMPASS BOTTLE

A Sculptural Water Bottle

Project Overview

This project is an exploration of form and emotion, resulting in an ergonomic water bottle designed for the mindful explorer. It moves beyond simple utility to become an inspirational companion for woodland walks and daily journeys.

Guided by a conceptual "word compass" of Happy, Inspirational, and Balance, the design process focused on translating these feelings into a physical form. The bottle is crafted for a specific user‚Äîa tall individual seeking a comfortable, secure, and beautiful hydration solution for hiking. Its unique, sculptural shape is designed to rest snugly against the user's leg and hip, while the integrated buckle allows for easy belt attachment.

The final design is a harmonious blend of materials and function, featuring a translucent glass body, a warm mahogany wood grip, and a hinged, magnetic cap for secure, one-handed operation.

Key Features & Design Process

The final form was the result of a rigorous, iterative design journey documented in a comprehensive process book.

From 2D to 3D: The core "squiggly line" concept was evolved through sketches, clay modeling, and advanced 3D modeling in Rhinoceros 7.

Iterative Refinement: The design went through four major versions, with each iteration solving specific problems identified during self-testing‚Äîimproving the grip, adjusting the size, refining the hinge, and ensuring stability.

Functional Details: The design incorporates a water-level viewing window, filleted edges for safety, and precisely engineered components like the magnetic "snap" closure to provide tactile and audible confirmation that the bottle is sealed.

Design Rationale

This project was a significant milestone in applying advanced free-form modeling techniques to translate an abstract emotional concept into a functional, highly-detailed product. It showcases a deep process of ideation, critical analysis, and iterative refinement.
