HUEY UH-1H

A Digital Reconstruction

Project Overview

A high-fidelity digital reconstruction of the iconic Bell UH-1 "Huey" helicopter, created at a 1:1 scale. This project is a deep dive into advanced surface modeling and the digital re-creation of complex mechanical assemblies.

The goal was to create a precise and detailed digital twin of the Huey, focusing on both its iconic external form and its intricate mechanical systems. The project demonstrates a mastery of advanced modeling techniques by balancing the helicopter's smooth, aerodynamic fuselage with the hard-surface precision of its engine and rotor components.

Key Challenges & Process

This model was meticulously built from the ground up in Rhinoceros 7, using historical blueprints to ensure dimensional accuracy.

Advanced SubD Modeling: The main fuselage was sculpted using Rhino's SubD tools, allowing for the creation of clean, flowing surfaces with precise topological control.

Mechanical Assembly: The greatest challenge was modeling the entire main rotor assembly, from the mast and swashplate down to the individual pitch control links, showcasing an ability to handle highly complex, interlocking parts.

Resilience & Problem-Solving: The process involved overcoming technical challenges, including recovering the model from a critical file corruption by integrating components from earlier versions, demonstrating perseverance and robust workflow management.

Design Rationale

This project is a testament to patience, precision, and the ability to deconstruct and digitally replicate a complex piece of engineering with an exceptional level of detail.
