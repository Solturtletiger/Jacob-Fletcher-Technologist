ATMOSPHERICA

Where Science Meets Art — An Environmental Simulator & Cloud Formation System

Project Overview

Atmospherica is a desktop device that brings the wonder of atmospheric science into the user's space by creating a real, visible cloud in a sealed glass container. Merging scientific principles with elegant design, it serves as both an interactive art piece and an engaging educational tool, transforming a complex natural phenomenon into a tangible, captivating experience.

Technologies & Tools

Arduino Used as the brain of the prototype, controlling all sensors and actuators to manage the delicate balance of temperature and humidity needed for cloud formation. Custom PCB Used to transition from a messy breadboard to a compact and reliable final product, integrating all electronic components onto a professional, purpose-built circuit board. Peltier Coolers Used as the core cooling technology with a stacked configuration to achieve the sub-zero temperatures necessary for rapid and visible cloud condensation. Rhino 8 Used for precise 3D modeling of all custom parts, ensuring a perfect fit and tolerance between the housing, internal components, and the glass container for a leak-proof assembly. Keyshot Used to create photorealistic product visuals for marketing, concept validation, and the final business proposal, effectively communicating the product's aesthetic and value. NumPy Used to power the physics simulation, performing high-speed numerical calculations for particle thermodynamics and kinematics, which was essential for accurately modeling the cloud formation process. Matplotlib Used to visualize the Python simulation data, creating dynamic 2D and 3D plots that clearly demonstrated the formation of the 'cloud zone' over time, validating the scientific concept before the physical build. Adobe Photoshop Used for post-processing renders to enhance realism, creating compelling composite images to visualize the cloud effect, and designing all presentation graphics to communicate the project professionally. DALL-E Used as a rapid ideation tool, generating a wide range of visual concepts for the product's form and lighting effects, which significantly accelerated the early-stage creative exploration. Gemini Used as a research and analysis assistant, helping to process scientific papers to refine the simulation's accuracy and to analyze user questionnaire data, providing key insights that shaped the final design.

My Role & Key Responsibilities

As the sole creator, I was responsible for the entire project lifecycle, from initial concept to a market-ready business plan. My key roles included:

Product Design & Strategy: Led research, defined the user experience, and developed the core product concept.

Hardware Prototyping & Engineering: Sourced components, designed custom parts, and built a fully functional prototype that solved key thermal and mechanical challenges.

UI/UX Design: Designed the physical interface, including the rotary dial controls and the custom pixel-art LCD screen layout.

The Process & Solution

The goal was to engineer a device that was both scientifically accurate and aesthetically beautiful.

Research-Driven Ideation: The process began with user questionnaires and AI-assisted concept generation (DALL-E) to define the product's form and function.

Rapid Prototyping & Problem-Solving: I engineered a functional prototype using stacked Peltier coolers to achieve sub-zero temperatures, a critical requirement for cloud formation. Custom parts were designed in Rhino 8 and validated with multiple 3D-printed iterations to ensure a perfect, leak-proof assembly.

Refined Design & User Interface: The final design features an elegant wood veneer housing and a custom-cut glass container. An intuitive UI with a rotary dial and LCD screen provides simple control over lighting, humidity, and atmospheric effects like "Golden Hour" or "Storm" modes.
